WHAT IS THIS PROJECT, IN ONE LINE?
----------------------------------------------------------------

It's a website that helps companies track how much money they are
spending on cloud services (like AWS, Google Cloud, Azure), and it
automatically detects problems, explains why they happened, and
suggests fixes.

Think of it like a smart accountant who watches your cloud bills
24/7 and shouts at you when something goes wrong.

----------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IS "THE CLOUD" ANYWAY?
----------------------------------------------------------------

When companies need computers, servers, or storage, they don't
always buy physical machines. Instead they rent them from big
companies like Amazon (AWS), Google (Google Cloud), or Microsoft
(Azure). This is called "the cloud."

These companies pay monthly bills based on how much they use.
Our project helps monitor and manage those bills.

----------------------------------------------------------------
WHO ARE THE COMPANIES IN OUR DATABASE?
----------------------------------------------------------------

We have 4 real sample companies in our system:

1. TechCorp India — a Technology company
2. Zomato Ltd — a Food Delivery company
3. Paytm Services — a Fintech company
4. Byju's Learning — an EdTech company

Each company has teams inside it, and each team has a monthly
budget for cloud spending.

WHAT DOES THE WEBSITE ACTUALLY LOOK LIKE?
----------------------------------------------------------------

The website has a dark themed dashboard with a sidebar on the left.
The sidebar has these pages:

1. Dashboard
2. Portfolio
3. Teams
4. Billing
5. Records
6. Analytics

Let's explain each one.

----------------------------------------------------------------
PAGE 1: DASHBOARD
----------------------------------------------------------------

This is the main page. When you open the website, this is what
you see first.

TOP TILES:
There are cards at the top showing each company's name and how
much total money they have spent. For example:
- TechCorp India: $18,100
- Zomato Ltd: $33,450
- Paytm Services: $26,500
- Byju's Learning: $5,300

You can also filter by month using the dropdown at the top right.
Select "January 2026" and all the numbers update to show only
January's spending. Select "Global Period (All Time)" to see
the total across all months.

COST DISTRIBUTION GRAPH:
Below the tiles is a smooth line graph showing how each company's
spending changed over time — from January 2026 to April 2026.
Each company has its own colored line. You can see who is spending
more, who is spending less, and how spending changed month by month.

INTELLIGENCE ALERTS (the panel on the right):
This is the smartest part of the dashboard. It automatically shows
three types of alerts, each in a different color:

RED ALERTS — Budget Breaches:
If a team has spent more than their monthly budget, a red alert
appears. Example:
"TechCorp India — Backend Team has exceeded monthly budget of
$5,000 with actual spend of $14,200"
This means Backend Team was given $5,000 to spend but spent $14,200.
That is a problem.

YELLOW ALERTS — Waste Detection:
If a company is paying a lot of money for a cloud service but
barely using it, a yellow warning appears. Example:
"Zomato Ltd is underutilizing Azure SQL — 854 hours usage but
$8,650 spend. Consider downsizing."
This means Zomato is paying $8,650 for Azure SQL but only using
it for 854 hours. They are wasting money on something they barely use.

BLUE ALERTS — Causal AI (Why did costs spike?):
If a team's spending suddenly jumped up compared to the previous
month, a blue alert explains WHY. Example:
"Cloud Storage costs for Content Team spiked 22% — likely due to
increased usage hours from 150 to 180 in March 2026."
This means the system automatically noticed the spike and explained
the reason using real data from the database. No human typed this —
the system figured it out on its own.

REMEDIATION SUGGESTIONS (below Intelligence Alerts):
This section shows actionable fix suggestions as cards. Example:
"Rightsize EC2 Compute instances for Backend Team"
"Enable auto-stop for Zomato Ltd Azure SQL"

Each card has an "Apply Fix" button. When someone clicks it,
the button turns grey and says "Acknowledged" — meaning a human
has seen the suggestion and noted it.

In a real production system, clicking Apply Fix would trigger
actual changes like sending an alert to the DevOps team, creating
a ticket, or calling the cloud provider's API to resize the server.
For this project, it simulates the acknowledgement step, which is
the first step in any remediation workflow.

RESOURCE GOVERNANCE TABLE (at the bottom of Dashboard):
A detailed table showing every team, their budget, their actual
spend, the difference (called drift), and a status badge.
If a team exceeded their budget, the status says EXCEEDED in red.

----------------------------------------------------------------
PAGE 2: PORTFOLIO
----------------------------------------------------------------

This page shows a high level overview of all companies in the system.

COMPANY CARDS:
Each company gets a card showing:
- Company name and industry
- Contact email
- Total amount invested (spent)
- Number of active teams
- Status badge: AT RISK (if any team exceeded 80% of budget)
  or HEALTHY (if all teams are within budget)

PORTFOLIO INVENTORY TABLE:
Below the cards is a sortable table with all companies listed.
You can click "Total Spend" to sort companies from highest
spender to lowest, or lowest to highest. A small arrow shows
which direction the table is sorted.

----------------------------------------------------------------
PAGE 3: TEAMS
----------------------------------------------------------------

This page has two things:

REGISTER NEW COMPANY form:
You can add a brand new company to the system by typing the
company name, industry, and contact email. It gets saved directly
into the MySQL database and immediately shows up everywhere
on the dashboard.

REGISTER NEW TEAM form:
You can add a new team under any company. You pick the company
from a dropdown, give the team a name, department, and monthly
budget. It gets saved to the database.

TEAM INVENTORY TABLE:
Shows all registered teams with their company, department,
and monthly budget.

----------------------------------------------------------------
PAGE 4: BILLING
----------------------------------------------------------------

This is where you log a new expense record.

You select a company, a team, a cloud service, and enter how
much was spent, how many hours were used, and which month the
bill is for. When you submit, it gets saved to the cost_records
table in MySQL.

This is how new spending data enters the system.

----------------------------------------------------------------
PAGE 5: RECORDS
----------------------------------------------------------------

This page shows every single expense record that exists in the
database. It's like a full ledger — a complete history of every
bill ever logged.

Each row shows the company, team, service, amount spent, usage
hours, and billing month.

There is also a Delete button on each row. If you made a mistake
while logging an expense, you can delete it from here and it
removes it from the database permanently.

----------------------------------------------------------------
PAGE 6: ANALYTICS
----------------------------------------------------------------

This page has two charts that give a visual breakdown of cloud
spending by provider and by service.

PROVIDER COST DONUT CHART:
A circular chart showing how much total money was spent on each
cloud provider — AWS vs Google Cloud vs Microsoft Azure.
The bigger the slice, the more was spent on that provider.

SERVICE UTILIZATION BAR CHART:
A bar chart showing spending broken down by individual cloud
service — EC2 Compute, Cloud Storage, Azure SQL, Lambda Functions,
BigQuery. You can see which specific services are costing the most.

Both charts respond to the month filter at the top. Select a
specific month and both charts update to show only that month's data.

HOW DOES THE TECHNICAL SIDE WORK?
----------------------------------------------------------------

Our project has three layers:

LAYER 1 — THE DATABASE (MySQL):
We have a database called "cloud_cost_monitoring" with 5 tables:
- companies: stores company info
- cloud_services: stores service names and providers
- teams: stores team info and monthly budgets
- cost_records: stores every expense ever logged
- alerts: stores budget breach alerts

LAYER 2 — THE BACKEND (Python Flask):
A Python program called app.py runs on port 5001.
It connects to MySQL and provides data through API endpoints.
These are like doors — the frontend knocks on a door and
gets data back. Our API doors include:
- /api/companies — gives company spending data
- /api/alerts — gives budget breach alerts
- /api/waste-alerts — gives idle/waste detection alerts
- /api/causal-alerts — gives causal AI spike explanations
- /api/remediation — gives fix suggestions
- /api/analytics — gives chart data
- /api/teams, /api/records, /api/months — other data

LAYER 3 — THE FRONTEND (HTML):
A single file called cloud_dashboard.html is the entire website.
It fetches data from the Flask API and displays it beautifully
using charts, tables, cards, and alerts.

To run the project:
1. Open Terminal
2. Type: cd ~/dbms_26
3. Type: source venv/bin/activate
4. Type: python app.py
5. Open cloud_dashboard.html in your browser

WHAT MAKES OUR PROJECT SPECIAL (THE 4 SMART FEATURES)?
----------------------------------------------------------------

Our teacher asked us to add 4 advanced features. Here is what
each one does and why it is impressive:

FEATURE 1 — PORTFOLIO PAGE:
A dedicated company overview page with status cards and a
sortable table. Shows which companies are "At Risk" vs "Healthy"
based on their teams' budget performance.

FEATURE 2 — IDLE/WASTE DETECTION:
The system automatically calculates a spending efficiency ratio
(amount spent divided by usage hours). If a company is spending
more than $10 per hour on a service, it flags it as wasteful and
shows a yellow warning in the Intelligence Alerts panel.
No human has to do this calculation — the database does it
automatically every time the page loads.

FEATURE 3 — AUTO REMEDIATION:
When budget breaches or waste is detected, the system doesn't
just complain — it suggests specific fixes. "Rightsize this
server." "Enable auto-stop for this service." Each suggestion
appears as a card with an Apply Fix button that can be
acknowledged by the team.

FEATURE 4 — CAUSAL AI:
This is the most impressive feature. The system compares each
team's spending month over month. If spending jumped by more
than 20%, it automatically writes a human-readable explanation
of why the spike happened, using real numbers from the database.
This is what real enterprise AI monitoring tools do — they don't
just detect problems, they explain them.
